Center (± standardize) X

📉 Residuals
⑥ Column Space Geometry ŷ ∈ col(X), ε ⊥ col(X)
y = ŷ + ε where ŷ = Z(ZᵀZ)⁻¹Zᵀ y (orthogonal projection onto col(Z)) ŷ ∈ col(Z) (ŷ is a linear combination of the PC score columns) ε = y − ŷ ⊥ col(Z) (residual is orthogonal to every column of Z) This means: Zᵀε = 0 (residuals uncorrelated with every predictor) And therefore: ŷᵀε = 0 (predicted values uncorrelated with residuals)
Geometric interpretation: The column space col(Z) is a k-dimensional plane in ℝⁿ. ŷ is the foot of the perpendicular from y to that plane. ε is the perpendicular itself — it points straight "up" out of the plane. The Pythagorean identity ‖y‖² = ‖ŷ‖² + ‖ε‖² gives us R² = ‖ŷ‖²/‖y‖².

📐 Pythagorean Decomposition of Variance
‖y − ȳ‖² = ‖ŷ − ȳ‖² + ‖ε‖² SST = SSR + SSE R² = SSR / SST = 1 − SSE/SST This decomposition holds because ε ⊥ (ŷ − ȳ) — a consequence of ε ⊥ col(Z)
